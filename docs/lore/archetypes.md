# Archetypes

A courier's **archetype** describes the background and culture they come from — the
life they lived before the underground recruited them, and the skills that background
gives them on the run. Archetype shapes stats, passive abilities, and the way other
crews read you on the street.

---

## Ninja

> *"You don't see them coming. You don't see them going. You find the package and wonder
> how it got there."*

**Origin:** Ninjas come from the City's shadow-ops ecosystem — former Axiom Dynamics
black-site contractors, corporate counter-intelligence agents who went freelance, or
street-trained operatives from Nightshade's more secretive crews. They were paid to
disappear and reappear somewhere they weren't expected. The courier underground offered
a way to keep doing exactly that, but on their own terms.

**Culture:** Ninjas don't advertise. They have no logos, no visible crew markings, no
distinctive gear. Their rep is built entirely through demonstrated performance —
delivers completed with zero trace evidence. Other couriers respect them deeply and
trust them with the most sensitive packages. They are, however, notoriously difficult
to book: they take the jobs they want and decline the rest without explanation.

**Strengths:** Maximum Stealth, elite Speed. Excels at surveillance-heavy districts
like The Grid and Airaway. Penalty: low Rep because they stay invisible.

## Punk Rocker

> *"Every run is a show. Every delivery is a statement. Every corp checkpoint we blow
> through is a verse in the song."*

**Origin:** The anti-corporate music and art underground of Nightshade. Punk Rockers
were buskers, graffiti artists, underground venue organizers, and scene architects
before they found the courier network. The underground gave them a way to channel
their fury into something that actually hurt the corps — moving the packages the corps
want stopped.

**Culture:** Loud, visible, and proud of it. Punk Rockers build their courier identity
as a public performance. They are the ones who tag corp surveillance cameras on the
way through, who broadcast their deliveries on encrypted feeds, who treat every
successful run as an act of rebellion. Their rep is enormous because everyone in
Nightshade knows their names.

**Strengths:** Maximum Rep, high Grit. Thrives in Nightshade and Glass City. Penalty:
low Stealth — they're not trying to hide.

## Ex Military

> *"Corps hired us to protect their assets. Turns out their biggest asset was us — and
> we quit."*

**Origin:** Discharged soldiers from Axiom Dynamics' private defense forces, or
veterans of the corporate border conflicts that the news feeds never covered. They
left the service for various reasons: moral line crossings they wouldn't cross, contracts
that ended badly, or simple exhaustion with serving a system that never valued them.
The courier underground valued exactly the skills the corps had built into them.

**Culture:** Disciplined and methodical. Ex Military couriers plan their routes the
way they planned operations — contingencies for every checkpoint, fallback routes
mapped in advance, gear maintained to spec. They work well with other crew members
and are often the ones who train new runners. Crews respect their operational
reliability even if their communication style can be a bit... formal.

**Strengths:** High Grit, balanced stats. Adaptable across all districts. Steady
performers rather than specialists.

## Hacker

> *"I don't need to know what's in the package. I built the encryption that's
> protecting it."*

**Origin:** Former Cascade Technologies employees, dark-web data brokers, and
self-taught intrusion specialists from The Grid's underground server farms. Hackers
entered the courier network because physical delivery is the only data channel that
Cascade's surveillance AI can't intercept. They carry chips they often encrypted
themselves — which also means they understand exactly how dangerous the information is.

**Culture:** Analytical and paranoid in equal measure. Hackers treat every run as a
security problem to be solved: identify the attack surfaces, eliminate the variables,
execute with precision. They rarely talk about their work, and when they do it sounds
like a different language. The Static Pack is almost exclusively made up of Hackers.

**Strengths:** Maximum Tech, strong Rep in digital-adjacent districts. The Grid is
their natural habitat.

## Chef

> *"I know every service entrance, every loading dock, every kitchen in this city.
> Turns out that's more useful than anyone thought."*

**Origin:** Workers from the City's massive food service infrastructure — chain
commissaries serving corp campuses, underground market vendors, private catering
staff for executive events. Chefs move through the City's service economy invisibly:
they have legitimate reasons to be in back corridors, freight elevators, and delivery
bays. The underground realized that a courier who looks like a catering delivery is
basically invisible to corp security.

**Culture:** Practical and sociable. Chefs are the most approachable archetype — they
know how to read a room, manage people, and charm their way through checkpoints that
would stop anyone else cold. They trade in favors and community goodwill as much as
scratch. In Batteryville and Nightshade markets, a well-connected Chef can access
back-routes that don't exist on any map.

**Strengths:** Good Speed, strong community networks, high terrain knowledge. Operates
well in any district but truly shines in Batteryville and Nightshade.

## Fash

> *"Reputation doesn't just open doors. In Glass City, reputation IS the door."*

**Origin:** Former insiders from Glass City's fashion and media industry — stylists,
brand managers, influencer coordinators, and event producers who burned their
corporate bridges but kept the connections and the access. Fash couriers can walk
through Glass City corridors that would get anyone else detained, simply because
Prism Media Group's facial-recognition index still shows them as friendly names.

**Culture:** Image-obsessed and strategically social. Fash couriers curate their
public persona as carefully as any Prism-sponsored celebrity. Their gear is always
polished. Their routes through Glass City are theatrical. They are the most visible
couriers in the network and have the highest baseline Rep of any archetype — but
they are also the most exposed. The corps watch beautiful things closely.

**Strengths:** Maximum Rep. Unmatched Glass City access. Limited Stealth — being
seen is part of the job.
